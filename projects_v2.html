AI-Powered Portfolio

A self-measuring portfolio that turns job applications into a trackable funnel.

Problem

Job applications usually disappear into a black box. I had no visibility into whether recruiters were opening my portfolio, revisiting it, or showing any real signal after I applied.

Solution

Built a FastAPI + PostgreSQL portfolio with application-specific ref links, recruiter visit logging, private analytics, time-on-site tracking, and a Groq-powered insight layer that surfaces patterns from real job-search data.

Impact

This project changed my portfolio from a brochure into a measurement system. I can now identify viewed applications, repeated visits, likely high-intent recruiters, and where to focus follow-ups instead of relying on guesswork.

My Role

End-to-end owner: product framing, data model design, backend APIs, Jinja frontend, dashboard logic, SQL analysis, GA4 event integration, cache strategy, security hardening, and deployment workflow.

Job Market Signal Analysis

An ongoing analysis project to understand which roles, skills, and positioning signals are actually worth prioritizing.

Problem

Applying broadly is not the same as applying intelligently. I wanted to understand which role categories, skills, and market signals were worth prioritizing instead of relying on generic job-board browsing.

Solution

Built an analysis repo focused on job-market signals so I can study demand patterns, role trends, and fit across the kinds of positions I am targeting. It complements the portfolio by helping me decide what to emphasize before I even apply.

Impact

This project reflects how I approach decisions: collect evidence first, then act. It supports better role targeting, stronger positioning, and more intentional project storytelling across applications.

My Role

Project owner and analyst. I framed the questions, structured the repo for ongoing market analysis, and used it as a working research layer to guide the rest of my application strategy.

Sales Analysis Dashboard

A Power BI dashboard built to surface sales, profit, customer, and regional performance in one decision layer.

Problem

Raw sales data rarely answers business questions by itself. Teams need a view of trends, segments, geographies, customer value, and profitability before they can decide where to focus.

Solution

Built a Power BI report on Superstore-style sales data to track total sales, profit, orders, customer metrics, top products, segment performance, regional trends, and city/state-level outcomes.

Impact

This project demonstrates the fundamentals I want hiring managers to see in analyst roles: KPI selection, slicing performance by business dimension, and turning operational data into a report someone could actually use.

My Role

I designed the dashboard structure, selected the business metrics, built the report in Power BI, and organized the repo so the PBIX, PDF output, and preview image all support the final story.
